Clickstream analysis is a method of analyzing the userâ€™s interactions with a website or digital product. It involves recording and analyzing the sequence of clicks made by users as they navigate through a website or app. The data collected can help to identify user behavior, patterns, and areas of improvement.

## When to use

Clickstream analysis can be used throughout the design and development process to gain insights into user behavior, track progress, and identify areas for improvement. It can be particularly useful for e-commerce sites and other transactional websites where optimizing the user flow is critical to achieving business goals.

<div class="mt-8 mb-8 grid grid-cols-2 divide-x overflow-hidden rounded-md border">
  <div class="py-6 px-10 space-y-4">
    **Pros**

    - Provides objective data on user behavior and patterns.
    - Helps to identify usability issues, such as dead-ends, errors, and confusion in the user flow.
    - Can help to improve conversion rates and other business metrics.
    - Provides a wealth of data that can inform design decisions and inform future iterations of the product.

  </div>
  <div class="py-6 px-10 space-y-4">
    **Cons**

    - Can be time-consuming to set up and analyze.
    - May not provide insights into the why behind user behavior.
    - Clickstream data can be misleading if not properly analyzed and contextualized.
    - May not provide insights into user sentiment or emotional responses to the product.

  </div>
